David Lawrence Miller

Biography

I am a postdoc at the Department of Natural Resources Science at the University of Rhode Island. At the moment my work is on the spatial distribution of seabirds off the coast of Rhode Island as well as developing statistical methods for spatial data. I'll be working with Scott McWilliams, Peter Paton, Jay Osenkowski and Kris Winiarski. I live and work in New York, NY (but I'm enjoying travelling around the USA; code).

Previous to that I was a postdoctoral research fellow working at the Centre for Research into Ecological and Environmental Modelling at the University of St Andrews.

From 2008-2011(12) I undertook a PhD at the University of Bath where I was investigating smoothing over complex regions under the supervision of Simon Wood. You can download and read my thesis should that interest you.

I have a first class (MMath) degree in statistics from the University of St Andrews, where I spent my summers eating cake working at CREEM.

I am a member of the National Centre for Statistical Ecology, Intenational Biometric Society, associate member of the Insitute of Physics and a fellow of the Royal Statistical Society and Institute of Mathematical Statistics.

I live in New York and enjoy reading, coding, cooking, science, Kubb, live music, building electronic things and photography. You can look at the things I find interesting on the internet on my tumblog.


Software

I write software. A lot of the software involves the package Distance, a Windows program for analysing distance sampling data. I'm part of a team of about 5 people around the world who develop the software. My work is mainly on the R components.

Most of my ongoing projects can be found on my github profile. Here is a (rough) list of software I've developed or helped develop.

Distance An R package (not to be confused with the Windows program) that allows one to perform simple distance sampling analyses. (Also available on CRAN.) This is basically just a more user friendly version of mrds...

mrds An R package that allows you to perform more complex analyses of distance sampling data (Including things like double observer studies. Jeff Laake is the package maintainer and I have contributed a fair amount of code since starting on the team in 2005. (mrds is available on CRAN.)

mmds An R package for performing distance analyses using mixture model detection functions. This formulation allows you to avoid non-monotonically decreasing detction functions, which usually cause bias in analyses. (The package can be found on CRAN too.)

msg A way of performing smoothing (using splines in a generalized additive model framework) over a geographical region with a complex shape (for example peninsulae or coastlines). The github page has an example of how to use msg. This is an implementation of the methods I developed as part of my PhD.
